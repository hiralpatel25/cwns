Documentation Rules

CWNS requires documentation to demonstrate the needs and costs for each facility/project.  In preparation for the CWNS 2008, EPA and the Data Submission and Review Methods Subcommittee (DSARMS) evaluated the documents used in previous surveys and some new documents to develop a list of pre-approved documents (Table 2-2; Appendix C provides definitions and specifies whether documents are pre-approved for documenting needs, costs, or both).  Document numbers were reorganized to group similar types of documents.  DSARMS also identified which of the pre-approved documents are footnotable. This list was validated by the CWNS Workgroup.  

 

For CWNS 2008, a facility/project’s documentation is considered footnotable if:

 

• The total needs are less than $20 million (2008 dollars)

 

• The document type is footnotable, as determined by the CWNS Workgroup (Table 2-2).  

 

Footnotable documents are retained in the State’s files, rather than being sent to EPA.  As with any other document, the name, author, and date must be entered into the CWNS database (Chapter 9). A portion of each State’s footnotable documents are audited (Chapter 17).

 

Remember: All documentation, redocumentation, and footnoting decisions should be made with costs in January 2008 dollars.

Cost Curves are available in the DEP to generate costs for facilities/projects with documented needs and insufficient cost documentation.  They are available for the following Facility Types: Treatment Plant, Clustered Systems (Decentralized Wastewater Treatment Systems), Onsite Wastewater Treatment Systems (Decentralized Wastewater Treatment Systems), Collection: Combined Sewers, and Collection: Separate Sewers.  (See Chapter 9 for more information.)

 

In addition to the pre-approved documents in Table 2-2, innovative methods can be used to document needs and costs with pre-approval from EPA.  The advantages of participating in the pre-approval process are:

 

• Submitted needs that follow the pre-approved methodology will be approved (Federal Accepted).

 

• Approved innovative approaches will be documented and shared with other States.  This can improve reporting nationally.

 

State CWNS coordinators should provide their proposed methodology for documenting needs and costs in a specific category to their EPA Regional CWNS Coordinator and EPA Headquarters staff (at cwns@epa.gov).  Proposal should include the following information:

 

• Category of need

 

• Whether the proposed methodology will be used for documenting needs and/or costs

 

• Description of proposed methodology (1-2 pages).  Attach sample surveys, spreadsheets, documentation, etc. when appropriate.  Describe any external data sets that you plan to use (e.g., land cover).

 

In February through May 2008, EPA staff will review proposed methodologies on a semi-monthly basis. The review will determine whether the proposed methodology meets CWNS eligibility requirements, CWNS documentation requirements, and SRF eligibility requirements. EPA will provide the State CWNS coordinator with feedback within two weeks.

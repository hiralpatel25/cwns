CWSRF Eligibility Audit

The CWSRF Eligibility Audit occurs in conjunction with both the Non-Footnotable Document Review and the Footnotable Documents Audit. The EPA contractor confirms that the State has submitted verification that it has a process in place to accurately assign the CWSRF eligibility percentages.  The EPA contractor verifies that CWSRF eligibility status designations are accurate by reviewing: (1) a 10% sample of non-footnotable documents (beginning October 1, 2008) and (2) audited footnotable documents (post-data entry).

 

During regular CWNS data entry, State Users identify the portion of each need that is CWSRF eligible, according to national CWSRF eligibility rules (Chapter 2).  For each item on which the EPA contractor disagrees with a State’s SRF % eligibility declaration, the EPA contractor summarizes the information by answering the following questions:

 

1. What is the facility/project in question?

 

2. What is the issue regarding CWSRF eligibility?

 

3. Why is the project not eligible?  (Citing CWSRF reference material or prior CWSRF Panel decisions)

 

The contractor sends this information to the CWSRF Panel for final eligibility decisions.

 

The CWSRF Panel consists of Stephanie VonFeck (EPA HQ CWSRF program), Michelle Tucker (CWSRF and CWNS Coordinator EPA Region 10), and Steve Vida (CWSRF Coordinator EPA Region 2).  

 

The CWSRF Panel meets monthly to consider the summary disagreements provided by the EPA contractor.  At monthly meetings, decisions are made about most disagreements forwarded by the contractor.  Items with a potential significant policy impact are elevated by the EPA Panel to EPA’s Office of Wastewater Management (OWM) management for a final decision. Following the monthly meetings, decisions are communicated to the Contractor, the State CWNS coordinator, and the State CWSRF coordinator.  If the CWSRF Panel denies eligibility, the State has one month to appeal the decision to the Panel.

 

After all decisions and appeals are finalized, the EPA contractor will complete an audit report for each State.  The report shows the percent of needs (by number of needs and needs dollar amounts) that States correctly identified as CWSRF eligible for each category.

Document Annotation

All documents (electronic and hard copy) submitted to the EPA contractor for review must be annotated as described below.  Submitted documents (and their corresponding needs) not complying with the below annotation guidelines will not be reviewed. States will be informed via e-mail if a document does not comply with the instructions and will be asked to resubmit the document.  

 

At a minimum, Adobe Reader is required to annotate documents within the DEP.  Free copies of Adobe Reader are available to download at http://www.adobe.com/.

 

Comments and highlights are stored separately from the document.  At the end of the survey, only the documents are shared, not the comments and highlights.  For this reason, electronic annotations should be done within the DEP.  Do not upload documents with annotations.

 

For documents submitted electronically:

 

• Click next to the Document Title in the Needs by Document data area to launch the EDMS  to view and annotate the document.  

 

• Use the Adobe Review and Comment functions to annotate the document.  A variety of tools, including highlight, note, callouts, arrows, and annotations are available.  In addition, comments can be added to any Markup by launching the comments area.  Click Show on the Comment & Markup toolbar.  A comment area is available for each markup (Figure 9-5).

 

Note: The Text Edits and Highlight Text tools cannot be used on scanned images.  Use the Rectangle or Line Tool

 

The annotate functions vary depending on the version of Adobe Acrobat loaded on the user’s computer. Use the Adobe Help function to get more information on the Comment and Markup functions.  

 

• Indicate the document title, date, and author. Comment must include the user’s initials; the facility/project’s CWNS number; and the type of information presented (e.g., KLF; 12000000981; Document Title) (Figure 9-5).

 

• Indicate the sections that contain the information describing:

 

• The water quality or public health problem(i.e., needs justification)

 

• The recommended alternative (if more than one is presented)

 

• Associated costs and category(ies) of costs.  Costs should be labeled with the same cost category(ies) entered into the DEP.

 

Comments must include the user’s initials, the facility/project’s CWNS number, and the type of information presented (e.g., need justification, recommended alternative, cost category).  For example: KLF, 12000000981, Need Justification; 12000000981, KFL, Category I cost.  

 

• Click to save and submit the annotations.  Before the annotation is submitted, a warning message appears and asks “are you sure you want to send your comments to the server?”  Click to save the annotations.  

 

For documents submitted in hard copy:

 

• Clearly label the document with the CWNS Number that applies to the document.  If a document is used as need or cost justification for more than one facility/project, each CWNS Number must be listed on the submitted document.

 

• Highlight the document title, date, and author.

 

• Highlight and flag the page or pages that contain the information describing:

 

• The water quality or public health problem (i.e. needs justification)

 

• The recommended alternative (if more than one is presented)

 

• Associated costs and category(ies) of costs.  Costs should be labeled with the same cost category(ies) entered into the DEP.

 

For documents supporting more than one facility/project, include the CWNS number on the flag.

 

• The following excerpts must be sent to the contractor: the cover page, a description of the water quality or health problem supporting the need, a description of the recommended alternative, and a summary of the costs for the recommended alternative.

 

Note: Complete documents are required for categories V and VII needs.

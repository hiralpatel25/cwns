Effluent Information

The data area Effluent Information (Figure 11-2) allows the user to indicate the effluent treatment level (Table 11-3) for facilities with the following Facility Types: Treatment Plant, Treatment Lagoon or Pond, and Storage Facility. Data can be entered for Present Treatment Level when the Status is present and for Projected Treatment Level when the Status is projected.

 

Remember: A warning message and the Feedback Status “Local Assigned” indicate that a Feedback copy exists for this facility/project.  When a Feedback copy exists, any changes made to the data area are reflected on the Feedback copy.  If a feedback copy has been returned (Feedback Status “State Review Requested”), this symbol indicates that a Feedback Fact Sheet includes changes to this data area.  Click it to view a pop-up box containing Feedback Fact Sheet.

Table 11- 3: Treatment Level Definitions

 

Treatment Level

Definition

Raw Discharge

Effluent that is not treated.

Primary Treatment  (45mg/l<BOD)

Process in which the effluent that is treated to remove floating debris and solids by screening and sedimentation.  

Advanced Primary

Process in which chemicals are added to further treat primary effluent and increase the amount of solid matter removed.

Secondary Wastewater Treatment

The minimum level of treatment required for discharges from all municipal wastewater treatment facilities by the Clean Water Act; facilities granted ocean discharge waivers under section 301(h) are exempt from this requirement (Appendix E).  The effluent must meet the minimum removal standards for Biochemical Oxygen Demand (Demand (less than or equal to 30 mg/l), total suspended solids (TSS), and pH in from municipal wastewater treatment facilities defined in Federal regulations.

Advanced Treatment

A level of treatment that is more stringent than secondary treatment or produces a significant reduction in nonconventional or toxic pollutants present in the facility’s effluent.  The treatment level is considered advanced if BOD is less than or equal to 20 mg/l or one or more advanced treatment indicators are in the facility’s permit.

 

 

• From the dropdown list, select one of the following Treatment Levels: Raw Discharge, Primary (45mg/l<BOD), Advanced Primary, Secondary, Advanced Treatment.

 

Note: Advanced Treatment should be selected if any of the listed Advanced Treatment Indicators are required in the permit (Figure 11-2).

 

• Check Disinfection if the facility uses a process (e.g. chlorination, UV radiation, ozone) to destroy or inactivate pathogens.  This option is available for all Treatment Levels.

 

• If Advanced Treatment is selected, the Advanced Treatment Indicators check boxes are enabled.  Indicate the facility’s Advanced Treatment Indicator(s) by checking its box. At least one Advanced Treatment Indicator is required.  

 

Nutrient Removal cannot be selected for CWNS 2008.  For facilities that had “nutrient removal” at the end of 2004, this box needs to be unchecked and a more specific indicator needs to be checked.  For most of these cases, EPA has pre-selected one or more of the specific indicators by examining the NPDES permit data.  States can accept or modify these pre-selections.

 

Check BOD as an Advanced Treatment Indicator only for levels less than or equal to 20 mg/L.  For all other indicators, check the box  if any limit is included in the permit.

 

If present and/or projected effluent is Raw Discharge/Primary/Advanced Primary and present and/or projected

Discharge method is Ocean Discharge, Outfall to Surface Waters, or Overland Flow, the user is warned that the present and/or projected Discharge is less than Secondary Treatment.  Only facilities with 301(H) Ocean Discharge Waivers can have a projected Discharge of less than Secondary Treatment (Appendix E contains a list of facilities with 301(H) waivers).

 

Click to save all changes to this data area. This will perform all error checking.  Error messages will appear if entered data does not meet all the business rules.  Click to return to the most recently saved version of the data.

 

Warning: If the user does not click before leaving this data area, entered data is not retained.

Data Reconciliation

At the end of the data entry period, the EPA contractor performs a quality assurance check to verify that the total population reported for a State does not exceed the ceiling EPA set at the beginning of the survey.  If the State population totals exceed the ceiling estimates, the EPA contractor will work with the State to resolve this discrepancy.   Population ceilings for 2008 and 2028 were developed from U.S. Bureau of the Census population estimates. Table 17-2 provides the present and projected population ceilings used for CWNS 2008.

 

The population ceilings are compared with the sum of the present resident population receiving treatment, present resident population serviced by decentralized wastewater treatment systems, and the present resident population not receiving collection. The 2028 projected population ceilings are compared with the sum of the future resident population receiving treatment, future resident population serviced by individual sewage disposal systems, and the future resident population not receiving collection.

 

Note: States should pay close attention to cities and towns that are served by more that one system. The population for the city/town must be divided among the systems to prevent double counting of population and potential problems at the end of the data entry period.

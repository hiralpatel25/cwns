CWSRF Eligibility

 

Clean Water State Revolving Fund (CWSRF) eligibility is no longer a requirement for CWNS eligibility.  For the first time, CWNS Official needs (formerly Federal needs) will include some facilities and projects that are not CWSRF eligible.  Prior to CWNS, each State is required to verify that they have a process in place for accurately assigning CWSRF eligibility.  During the survey, the State identifies the portion of each need that is CWSRF eligible (see Chapter 9).

 

For the purposes of the CWNS, a CWSRF eligible need is one that is eligible for funding according to national CWSRF eligibility rules.  The CWSRF program is managed largely by the States, and CWSRF eligibility varies according to each State's program and priorities.  In order to have data that is consistent and comparable nationally, any facility/project that is eligible for CWSRF funding according to national rules, regardless of an individual State’s eligibility rules, should be designated as CWSRF eligible in the CWNS database.

 

The following resources are available to assist States to determine what needs are CWSRF eligible:

 

• The recorded web seminar “National State Revolving Fund (SRF) Eligibility Rules” presented by Stephanie VonFeck (EPA CWSRF program) is available at http://www.epa.gov/cwns/record.htm

 

• State CWSRF personnel.  Refer to the process that the State established prior to data submittal.

 

• National CWSRF documentation (from EPA CWSRF Branch), copied into the CWNS Portal Library for easy access (Chapter 4)

 

• EPA’s CWSRF Regional Coordinators, who may consult with the Headquarters CWSRF Branch

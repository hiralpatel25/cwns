2008 Goals & Objectives

In preparation for CWNS 2008, EPA and the States continued to work together to make the data entry system, data quality, and data accessibility even better.  Together, they developed the following goals and objectives:

 

Goal 1: Make it easier for States and local communities to submit information for CWNS 2008

 

Objective 1-1 – Move the CWNS data entry system to the Internet to enable easier system access by any State user, including other State program staff and other State agencies’ staff.  Extend data entry capabilities to facilities and other local parties.

 

Objective 1-2 – Reduce the amount of data to be updated by integrating the CWNS data entry system with other EPA data, including National Permit Discharge Elimination System (NPDES) data in Permit Compliance System (PCS) / Integrated Compliance Information System (ICIS); Clean Water State Revolving Fund Benefits (CBR) data; Impaired Waters and TMDL data in the Assessment, TMDL Tracking And ImplementatioN System (ATTAINS), and location data in WATERS Lite Viewer (WLV).

 

Objective 1-3 – Integrate CWNS document management with EPA’s developing Electronic Document Management System (EDMS) for ability the to electronically submit documents, link documents to CWNS data records, and access documents in EDMS related to other EPA programs.

 

Goal 2: Update CWNS methods and procedures to improve survey accuracy and utility

 

Objective 2-1 – Promote EPA’s Sustainable Infrastructure (SI) Initiative efforts by evolving CWNS so that it estimates 20-years needs (including gathering basic pipe inventory and replacement schedule data); contains more current and complete treatment technology data; and can help assess progress in facilities implementing utility management programs.

 

EPA’s SI Initiative was launched in 2005 in response to EPA's 2002 Clean Water and Drinking Water Gap Analysis Report.   EPA is collaborating with drinking water and wastewater utility managers, trade associations, local watershed protection organizations, and State and local officials to help ensure that our nation’s precious water infrastructure is sustainable in the future.  The four priority areas, or pillars, are: (1) Better Management; (2) Full Cost Pricing; (3) Watershed Approach; and (4) Water Efficiency.  For more information, see http://www.epa.gov/waterinfrastructure.

 

Objective 2-2 – Review NPS needs documentation rules to ensure consistency with point source needs documentation rules and accurate documentation of NPS needs.

 

Objective 2-3 – Maximize the participation of local communities and States in submitting information to CWNS 2008.

 

Goal 3: Improve CWNS data access

 

Objective 3-1 – Make all CWNS data accessible in Ask WATERS. (http://www.epa.gov/waters/tools/ask_waters/cwns_query_tool.html)

 

Objective 3-2 – Design and distribute local and State fact sheets that summarize CWNS data in easy to understand formats.
